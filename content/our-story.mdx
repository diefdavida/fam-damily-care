I am a remote, secondary caregiver.  

The amount of time I'm able to support mom and dad is included somewhere in with data on a staggering number of families like mine: In 2024 there were 59 million family caregivers in the U.S. who were caring for other adults. [(Link)](https://www.aarp.org/caregiving/financial-legal/valuing-the-invaluable-report-2026/)

I am fortunate enough to be able to visit my parents once a month for a few days, as they both adapt to new chapters in their very different aging journeys, but most of what I can do is remote.  

I explore services and technology that can help me cross the miles even when I can’t be there in person.

**If you landed here**, chances are you are living far enough away from your loved ones that frequent visits aren’t likely.

**If you landed here**, chances are you’ve felt some degree of guilt for not being able to ‘do more’ even while not knowing what ‘more’ is.

**If you landed here**, you likely love your whole damn family as much as I love mine- welcome to this “Damn Family” within Fam Damily Cares.
Aging isn’t optional, but our perceptions of growing older, and the ways we support aging in our families and communities, can have a major impact on how it feels.

There is strength in family and in community, whether in person or virtually.  

There is even more strength in knowing an experience that is new to you may represent a road someone else has travelled.

**If you landed here now**, you may also need something, anything, to hang your hope on.  I have a lot more to share about our own journey (check out more here), but for now here is my offering:

## What do you do now that your loved one needs support and care and you feel far away?

**Talk to friends**- You will be surprised, perhaps, at how many can related.  Because we don’t talk about aging much, we don’t share our stories enough.

**Find a support group**-  I started one we call Dementia and Margaritas (**DAM**, for short) with a small group of caregivers with loved ones who have brain change.  In person works best for me.  

**Investigate technology solutions**- I’ll be doing some of that here and sharing as well.  There are A LOT of solutions coming up and I’ll evaluate what works for me and may family. But to be clear, no tech will ever replace a physical hug from mom.  

**Don’t Should all over yourself**-  This will be a judgement free area.  People will tell you that you should have planned years ago for this.  That isn’t helpful because you are here, now.  Don’t listen to the ‘should haves’, but rather embrace that ‘next best step for us’ mentality.

**Reach out to me**: This is a place for me to share but also to learn.  Help me help us all more but allowing me to listen and hear your own stories.

## What you'll find here

**Family Needs** — A plain-language look at the two big caregiving paths: aging in place and community-based care. Not advocacy for one over the other — just what's actually involved in each, and what to watch for.

**Tech Discovery Guide** — An overview of the categories of technology that exist for aging adults and caregivers. Not reviews — just a map of what's out there so you know what to search for.

**Reviews** — First-person accounts of specific products and tools I've actually used. I write about what problem I was trying to solve, what worked, and what didn't. No sponsored content.
