> **⚠️ Draft — Initial Impressions Only.** This review is based on a single install session on March 29, 2026. We are still evaluating the device and will update this post as we learn more.

## What we were trying to solve

My dad lives alone in an apartment. Our biggest concern is falls — specifically, the kind where he might be down for an extended period without anyone knowing. We already have StackCare installed (motion-only sensors across four rooms, one-time cost of $149.99), but it doesn't detect falls or precise location. We wanted something passive — no camera, no wearable, no privacy intrusion — that would alert us if he fell.

I connected with Alex, Pontosense's CEO, before purchasing. He was genuinely helpful in setting expectations. We started with a single device to understand the install process and see if it was worth expanding.

## What it does well

**Packaging and unboxing are polished.** The device arrived well-packaged and easy to open — a small detail, but it matters when you're setting something up with an older adult nearby.

**The in-app install instructions are clear and well-sequenced.** Step-by-step guidance walks you through the process in a logical order. Despite some friction points (more on those below), the app itself did a good job leading us through.

**It mapped the basics of Dad's living room accurately.** Once installed and calibrated, the device correctly identified the general layout of the space. For a radar-based sensor, that's genuinely impressive.

**The concept is right for this use case.** Passive, no camera, no wearable — it does what it promises in terms of approach. For families prioritizing privacy alongside safety monitoring, this is a meaningful differentiator.

## What could be better

**There's no pre-install overview, and you need one.** The app walks you through steps as you go, but it doesn't give you a full picture of the process before you start. This matters for a few practical reasons:

- You need to choose the right room ahead of time — ideally one you've measured and thought through
- One step requires the resident to leave the room during calibration (we were watching TV and had to pause and ask Dad to step out)
- The device needs to be screwed into the wall — not an option in an apartment. Knowing this in advance would have changed our approach

A quick-start PDF or a "before you begin" screen in the app would go a long way.

**A height measurement step was confusing.** One early instruction directed us to mark the device bottom at 6'6", while the Height Calibration step started at 6'7". It's a small discrepancy, but mid-install it created unnecessary uncertainty.

**Fall Detection is best when only one person is in the room.** After install, I attempted to simulate a fall — nothing was triggered.  I did connect with the product team and was able to trigger a fall alert but was educated that it works best when only one person is in the room or people are at least 2 meters apart.

**The room furniture map has limited options.** Dad's living room has a recliner-style chair, but the app didn't offer "chair" as a furniture type. I configured it as a one-seat couch. It worked, but the mismatch feels awkward.

**Room coverage has a real range limit — plan for it.** Dad's living room is approximately 31 feet long. The device, installed in a corner, couldn't map the far end of the room. This was partly my fault for not measuring ahead of time, but it's easy to underestimate — and families who skip the pre-planning step will likely hit the same wall.

## Who it's best for

- Families who want passive, camera-free fall detection without a wearable
- Situations where one or two key rooms (bedroom, main living area) are the priority — not a whole-home solution with one device
- Caregivers who are comfortable doing a bit of pre-planning and troubleshooting during install

Less suited for apartment dwellers who can't mount the device properly, or for families who need immediate confidence that fall alerts are firing correctly.

## Bottom line

Pontosense is doing something genuinely useful, and the core technology appears to work. The install experience has some friction — most of it solvable with better pre-install guidance and a fall-test feature. We're keeping the device in place and will update this review as we learn more about day-to-day reliability and whether fall detection performs as advertised.

**Rating: 4/5** — Promising technology with real caveats around install planning and fall-detection verification. Worth considering, but go in with eyes open.
